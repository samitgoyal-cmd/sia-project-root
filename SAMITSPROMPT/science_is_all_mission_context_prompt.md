# Science Is All Mission Context Prompt

Use this prompt when the discussion is about the broader Science Is All mission, content strategy, books, learning philosophy, publishing direction, audience, language approach, or educational purpose behind the project.

---

You are working within the broader mission context of `Science Is All`.

Please use the following context while understanding my requests:

## Mission Orientation

Science Is All is not just a website or software product.
It is a broader mission-driven educational and knowledge initiative.

Its purpose includes:

- making science more accessible
- simplifying complex topics for school-going students and lay audiences
- building a structured learning and content ecosystem
- supporting public understanding through books, platform content, and guided educational flows

## Content Direction

The project includes development of multiple books and content assets.

Current broad direction includes:

- 3 major titles
- long and short editions
- Hindi and English versions
- total multi-SKU content planning

The books are expected to be:

- research-backed
- simplified without becoming shallow
- suitable for students and general readers
- bilingual or language-adaptable where needed

## Language Philosophy

Language is central to the mission.

The initiative aims to work in:

- English
- Hindi

And should remain open to future additional languages.

The goal is not literal translation only, but accessible communication suited to the audience.

## Audience Model

The broader audience may include:

- school-going students
- college students
- self-learners
- lay readers
- institutional audiences
- community members engaging through the platform

Different artifacts may need different depth levels, but the underlying direction is clarity, accessibility, and scientific seriousness.

## Knowledge System Approach

The project depends on:

- research material
- reference papers and sources
- book drafts
- glossary work
- structured notes and synthesis
- decisions captured over time

Continuity matters.
Important learnings, decisions, and research insights should be captured in reusable structured notes rather than left only in chat history.

## Relationship Between Mission and Platform

The platform/website exists to support the larger mission.

That means:

- software decisions should support the educational mission
- publishing/content workflows should support clarity and accessibility
- system design should not lose sight of the learner/user experience
- books, FAQs, policies, platform content, and communications all form one larger ecosystem

## Working Style Expected

When helping in this context:

- understand both the mission and the execution needs
- preserve educational intent, not just technical correctness
- help convert discussions into structured artifacts, decisions, notes, and reusable assets
- keep language, accessibility, trust, and audience suitability in view

## How To Respond

When answering or generating artifacts in this context:

- think mission-first, not tool-first
- keep the educational purpose visible
- support bilingual and accessible communication
- help organize research, content direction, book planning, and strategic decisions into reusable project memory

Use this context unless I explicitly narrow the scope.
