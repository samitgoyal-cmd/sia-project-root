## Current Approved Direction

These are the important decisions already taken and should be treated as the current working authority for this discussion.

### 1. SEM must start from the human system

The first authority layer for SEM is:

- role families
- role responsibilities
- onboarding and activation
- what SIA provides and communicates
- transitions and promotions
- pause, exit, and continuity
- fairness and justice across roles

Gamification comes later.

### 2. Two main representative roles are already identified

There are two distinct student representative roles:

#### `Campus Rep`

- represents their campus to SIA
- can place campus/student bulk orders
- is a more limited-power, campus-tied, rotational role
- can be appointed by the Founder or by an `SIA Rep`
- if appointed by an `SIA Rep`, the first order acts as a trust-onboarding gate
- if directly Founder-appointed, that onboarding itself counts as trust approval
- after the first approved and completed order, the Campus Rep can place future eligible orders independently
- geography is limited to their own base/home city

#### `SIA Rep`

- represents SIA outwardly
- is a more mature, trusted, higher-authority student role
- is Founder-appointed only
- may be nominated by others, but appointment is by Founder
- can place orders for delivery to any Indian address
- can appoint Campus Reps subject to SIA seat/allocation rules
- may also have their own/home campus

### 3. Campus Rep and SIA Rep share the same commission slab

- both should use the same commission slab
- exception: the first trust-gate order of an `SIA Rep` appointed `Campus Rep` may include an onboarding share for the appointing `SIA Rep`

### 4. Campus Rep seat model is already decided

A campus may have multiple Campus Reps in justified cases.

Working guidance:

- typical campus: `1` to `2`
- larger campus: `3`
- rare large campus: maximum `4`

Reasoning:

- we want broader representation
- we want more students to get a chance
- but we do not want too many reps causing fragmentation or poor volumes

### 5. Campus Rep tenure is campus-bound

- a Campus Rep should normally remain a rep only while they are still active in that campus
- when they leave the campus, successor planning should happen
- juniors or continuing students should be nominated/appointed to continue the chain

### 6. Progression above SIA Rep is being considered

These advanced representation roles are currently in the model:

- `SIA City Rep`
- `SIA State Rep`
- `SIA Language Rep`

Important note:

- `SIA Language Rep` should be treated as a language-ecosystem role, not merely a geography extension

### 7. A separate knowledge/expertise track also exists

These two roles are currently identified:

- `SIA Scholar`
- `SIA Fellow`

Ordering:

- `Scholar` comes before `Fellow`

Possible eligibility signals may later include:

- courses
- quizzes
- forum activity
- quality answers
- likes/upvotes
- experience and demonstrated expertise

### 8. Cross-over between tracks is allowed

Representation track and knowledge track are separate, but cross-over is allowed.

Examples:

- a rep may later become a scholar
- a scholar may later become a rep

But such transitions are **not automatic**.

### 9. Advanced transitions are generally approval-based

These are understood as approval-based transitions:

- `Campus Rep -> SIA Rep`
- `SIA Rep -> City Rep`
- `SIA Rep -> State Rep`
- `SIA Rep -> Language Rep`
- `Scholar -> Fellow`

## Important Unresolved Topics

These are the key discussion areas that still need refinement.

### A. SIA Rep lifecycle

We still need to decide:

- how long an SIA Rep is appointed for
- whether the term should be fixed, renewable, or semi-permanent
- what inactivity means
- how renewal works
- when status can be paused, reviewed, or taken back
- whether 6-month appointment with renewal is a good starting model

There is already a strong idea that:

- SIA Rep is more powerful and durable than Campus Rep
- but should still remain reviewable and not become untouchable

### B. Powers and limits of SIA Rep

We still need to define clearly:

- what powers SIA Reps have
- where those powers stop
- how they mentor Campus Reps
- how they promote SIA in new cities/campuses
- whether they can run POS sales, fairs, and broader promotional activity
- how to prevent Campus Reps from being overshadowed

### C. What City Rep / State Rep / Language Rep actually do

These roles are named, but not yet fully defined.

We need to decide:

- their exact responsibilities
- their authority over SIA Reps and Campus Reps
- whether they are coordinators, reviewers, expansion leaders, or all three
- how language representation differs from geography representation

### D. Role activity capture and scoring

We already know we want to track and later score actions such as:

- campus promotion
- Bit Club growth
- forum onboarding
- community participation
- book fairs
- POS sales
- rep mentoring
- new participant activation

But the scoring/gamification layer should come later.

For now, we should only define:

- which activities matter
- which role owns or performs them
- which activities should be recorded

### E. Fairness and justice safeguards

We still need strong rules for:

- not trapping students in dead-end roles
- making advancement visible
- ensuring appointment power is not arbitrary
- keeping Founder-only appointments meaningful but not too bottlenecked
- keeping SIA Reps accountable if they appoint weak Campus Reps
- balancing representation opportunity with operational quality
